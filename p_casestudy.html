Penny App Case Study

The Problem

People use many different apps when arranging their travel plans.

The Solution

Penny is an app that offers curated travel experiences with comprehensive reviews in one place.

My Role

UX/UI Designer (Individual Project)

Timeline

4 weeks

The Process

Research

Definition

Ideation

Prototyping

Testing

Conclusion

Research

Protopersona

For this research project, I wanted to understand what tools users prefer when planning their vacation including how they selected their destination, scheduling their travel plans, and setting their itenerary.

I planned and conducted six 1-on-1 interviews for potential users to find out how they felt about the tools they used to make their travel plans.

From the interviews conducted, I created an affinity diagram to organize my research. I found that average amount of trips planned for the calendar year were between 3 and 4 depending on their schedule. I also found that sticking to a budget for their trip was very important.

Empathy Map
User Persona

From my research, I was able to create an empathy map and also a user persona. Meet Abigail.

Abigail is a middle school teacher who is organized and budget conscious. She is passionate about seeing new places and spending time outdoors. She needs to read all of the reviews when she plans trips with her friends to help make the best decision and stay in budget. She loves when she can afford to stay at a super cute location so she can post pictures to her Instagram feed.

Definition

User Insight

A budget conscious traveler needs access to comprehensive reviews when preparing their itinerary because they want to confirm they are selecting the best experiences available when visiting a new location.

Problem Statement

We have observed that current reviews lack reliable information for budget conscious travelers who are looking for new experiences to enjoy at their destination. Since their budget limits the experiences they can participate in, they want peace of mind knowing they made a good choice.

Value Proposition

Penny offers curated travel experiences with comprehensive reviews.

Feature Prioritization

I focused on finding the features that would be important for the user when using this app. How might we help a budget conscious traveler who needs to make informed decisions find a substantial amount of accurate, compelling, and highly detailed reviews from trusted sources?

Ideation

Storyboard

This storyboard walks through how the user would use two of the features in the app. First feature is having awards given to experiences that have a lot of positive reviews by other users. The second feature is seeing lists of experiences by budget.

User Scenario

Features chosen: 1. I like how TripAdvisor gives awards to their experiences on the type of reviews. I can trust these. 2. I wish there was a way to see inspiration by budget.

Prototyping

I completed a competitor analysis on two direct competitors and 3 indirect competitors. More information on what I learned here.

Sketches

I created sketches to figure out what the app would look like. I used pen and paper to get ideas down on what I was thinking for the way to move through the app. InVision Prototype.

Lo-Fi Wireframes

I then created a low to mid fidelity wireframe from my sketches. This set of wireframes I created in Adobe XD and was able to prototype and utilize for my user tests. Adobe XD Prototype.

Testing

Guerrilla testing plan and notes. Talk about the 4 different tasks to just have the user move through the app to make sure it could funciton.

I conducted 4 user tests with 4 tasks total to complete.. My focus was to make sure that the flow of the app made sense to the user and was easy to understand. Only 50% of the tasks were completed. Another paragraph here about what I learned from my user tests.

Updated Lo-Fi Wireframes

After half of my tasks failed in the user tests, I made iterations to the wireframes to make them better. Some of these updates include a search option and opting for a different page for you to enter the app as the home screen.

Conclusion

Hi-Fi Mockups

Key Findings

Having the features available for users to research and create itineraries is beneficial. Keeping it simple and not overcomplicating it for the user is the ideal situation. I learned a lot about how users flow through the app. I feel like this is something that could take a lot longer to do with all of the information and testing that is involved to make it a great product.

Next Steps

Continue to iterate on prototype. There are lots of things that can be done to make it much better than it currently stands.
